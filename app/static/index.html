Case study

From classroom frustration to structured product thinking

01

The problem

Teachers spend significant unpaid time adapting activities for age, English level, reading ability, writing ability, pencil control, lesson length, and individual support needs.

02

The product idea

LessonBuddy turns those constraints into an explicit brief, proposes a timed lesson structure, and separates teacher guidance from student-facing materials.

03

The quality approach

Generated output is not treated as correct by default. The system is designed around validation, repair, teacher review, and regression testing before materials are considered publishable.
